1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you create a Plenee account, we collect:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Password (stored as a one-way hash — we never store your plaintext password)
  • Phone number (optional — collected only if you enable two-factor authentication via SMS)

1.2 Financial Data via Plaid

Plenee uses Plaid Technologies, Inc. ("Plaid") to connect to your financial institution accounts. When you link a bank account, Plaid provides us with:

  • Bank account and routing numbers
  • Account balances and account type
  • Transaction history (amounts, dates, merchant names, categories)
  • Account holder name as reported by your financial institution

Plaid's own collection and use of your information is governed by Plaid's Privacy Policy and Plaid's End User Privacy Policy. We encourage you to review those policies.

1.3 Usage, Device, and Technical Signals

We automatically collect technical information when you use the Service in order to personalize your experience — for example, to tailor the layout, defaults, and financial insights we show you based on your device, location, and how you interact with the app. This includes:

Log & Network

  • IP address, timestamp, and error reports
  • Geographic location derived from your IP address (city, region, and ZIP code — we do not collect GPS coordinates)
  • Internet service provider and connection type (residential, mobile, or corporate network)

Device & Browser

  • App version and device operating system
  • Device type, screen dimensions, and display density
  • Device memory tier and CPU core count (used as device quality signals for UI optimization)
  • Browser type and language preferences
  • Timezone

Connection & Session

  • Network connection quality (connection type and estimated speed)
  • Whether data-saver mode is active on your device
  • Referral source — the website or campaign that directed you to Plenee (including UTM campaign parameters from marketing links)
  • In-app interaction patterns used in real time to personalize the experience (e.g., which features you engage with first); these real-time signals are processed in memory and are not stored or logged

1.4 Inferences

We may derive inferences from the information described above to personalize your experience. For example, we may estimate a general income range based on your ZIP code using publicly available Census data, or infer device quality from hardware signals, in order to tailor the presentation of the Service. These inferences are used solely to improve your experience and are not used for credit scoring, advertising targeting, or any purpose unrelated to the Service.

We do not use third-party advertising trackers or sell usage data to analytics providers.

4. Financial Privacy Notice (GLBA)

Federal law (15 U.S.C. § 6801 et seq., the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act) requires us to explain how we collect, use, and protect your personal financial information. This notice applies to current and former Plenee customers.

What information we collect

We collect the following types of personal financial information to provide the Service: bank account and routing numbers, account balances, transaction history, and account holder name as reported by your financial institution.

How we use your information

We use your personal financial information solely to operate and improve the Plenee Service — to display your accounts, generate budgeting insights, and send you account-related notifications. We do not use your financial information for marketing to you on behalf of third parties, and we do not provide it to data brokers, marketing companies, or nonaffiliated businesses for their own purposes.

Infrastructure service providers

To deliver the Service, we use infrastructure providers (Plaid, Supabase, Render, Vercel) who process your data on our behalf under contract. These providers are not permitted to use your data for any purpose other than providing services to Plenee.

When we are legally compelled

We will provide your information to government or regulatory authorities only when required to do so by a valid court order or subpoena. We will not voluntarily provide your financial information to law enforcement absent such compulsion.

5. Data Retention

We retain your account and financial data for as long as your Plenee account is active. If you delete your account:

  • Your account information is deleted within 30 days
  • Your financial transaction data is deleted within 30 days
  • Your bank account connection is disconnected from Plaid immediately upon account deletion
  • Encrypted backup copies are purged within 90 days

We may retain limited records longer where required by law (e.g., records of transactions for tax or regulatory compliance purposes).

6. Data Security

We protect your information using industry-standard security controls:

  • Encryption in transit: All data is transmitted over TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encryption at rest: All data stored in our database is encrypted at rest using AES-256 (Supabase infrastructure encryption)
  • Plaid token protection: Plaid access tokens are additionally encrypted at the application layer before storage — they are never stored in plaintext
  • Access control: Employee access to production systems requires multi-factor authentication; access is limited to personnel who need it to perform their role
  • No local data storage: We do not permit employees to download your financial data to local devices

No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure. If you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately at security@plenee.com.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Account Deletion

You may delete your Plenee account at any time from within the app. Upon deletion, we will disconnect all linked bank accounts and delete your data as described in Section 5.

Data Access

You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you by contacting us at privacy@plenee.com. We will respond within 30 days.

Bank Account Disconnection

You may disconnect any linked bank account at any time from within the app. This removes our access to new transaction data from that account; historical transaction data already retrieved will be deleted within 30 days of account disconnection.

California Residents (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect and how it is used
  • Delete your personal information
  • Opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information (Plenee does not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising)
  • Non-discrimination for exercising your rights

Categories of personal information we collect (California disclosure):

  • Identifiers (name, email address, IP address, phone number)
  • Financial information (bank account numbers, transaction history, balances)
  • Device and internet activity information (device signals, usage patterns, referral source)
  • Geolocation data (city and ZIP code derived from IP address)
  • Inferences drawn from the above (estimated income range, device quality tier)

To exercise your California rights, contact us at privacy@plenee.com.

8. Age Requirement

The Service is intended for adults aged 18 and older. You must be at least 18 years of age to create an account or use the Service. This requirement exists because using the Service requires entering into a binding legal agreement and connecting financial accounts that require adult ownership under applicable banking and contract law.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a person under 18, please contact us at privacy@plenee.com and we will delete it promptly.

9. Cookies and Consent-Based Tracking

The core Plenee Service operates without requiring cookies. Technical signals described in Section 1.3 are collected passively as part of serving the application and do not require a cookie consent mechanism.

The following features, if and when implemented, will require your consent via a cookie notice before activation:

  • Returning visitor recognition — remembering your preferences across sessions
  • Saved estimator or onboarding state — persisting your inputs between visits
  • Third-party marketing pixels (e.g., Meta Pixel, TikTok Pixel) — used only if Plenee runs paid advertising campaigns; these would be disclosed and gated behind consent
  • Session recording tools (e.g., Hotjar, FullStory) — if implemented for UX research

You will be notified and given the opportunity to consent before any of these are enabled. You may withdraw consent at any time by contacting privacy@plenee.com.

Continued use of the Service after the effective date of a revised policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms.
